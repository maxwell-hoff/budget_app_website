The fix

From messy transactions to a number you trust — and the tools that get you there.

Here's how Workbench fixes that. A guided setup walks you in from zero — plain-language questions, no blank screen, your real numbers in about five minutes — and from there you're in a loop. Each step below is a stage; the tools beneath it are what you use to get through it, every one built to show you the downstream price of a decision before you commit. Not just what you spent, but what it compounds to.

Our philosophy

Understanding can't be handed to you.

There's a Taoist idea that the knowledge in a craftsman's hands can't be passed on in words — it has to be worked for. Finances are the same. You can read reports, get advice, follow a plan. But knowing what your money is doing and where it's taking you only comes from working with it directly. So I built a tool that rewards that — clean accounting underneath, scenario planning on top, variance analysis to catch the quiet drains.

One person. Ten years.

Workbench started as a spreadsheet built on a Mint export — the kind you build because nothing else does what you need. Ten years and a few hundred iterations later, it became an app. Built by one person, in the Midwest. I use it every month and it changed how I think about my own finances — I built this hoping it does the same for you. I'm now building the next chapter in public — follow along at @GardenStudio__ on Twitter.
