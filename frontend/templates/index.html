Why Workbench exists

Most budgets don't fail from a lack of discipline. They fail because you can't see what breaking them costs you.

So we built a tool that makes the cost of every choice visible — and lets you arrive at your budget instead of being handed one.

Overspend on dining this month and nothing pushes back. There's no signal that the $300 you didn't save just quietly became a few thousand you won't have in five years. The decision and its consequence are separated by years — so in the moment, the real cost stays invisible.

That's the actual reason rules of thumb don't work. 50/30/20, "spend less than you make," the envelope method — none of them can show you the downstream price of a single decision, because none of them are tied to your trajectory. So discipline feels arbitrary, and the budget quietly dies.

Who it's for

You've already outgrown Mint, YNAB, and Monarch. You don't need help categorizing your coffee — you want to genuinely understand your numbers and steer them. But you're not a financial planner with a client book and a CFP, and you shouldn't have to become one. Workbench sits exactly there: serious enough to model real trade-offs, plain enough to actually use.

How it works

A budget you arrive at — not one you're handed.

Rules of thumb don't know your life. Workbench turns your own numbers into a budget you can trust — each step building on the last, until the right figure is just sitting there in front of you.

Our philosophy

Understanding can't be handed to you.

There's a Taoist idea that the knowledge in a craftsman's hands can't be passed on in words — it has to be worked for. Finances are the same. You can read reports, get advice, follow a plan. But knowing what your money is doing and where it's taking you only comes from working with it directly. So we built a tool that rewards that — clean accounting underneath, scenario planning on top, everything on your machine.
