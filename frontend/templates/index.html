Overwhelmed by your finances? It's not you, it's your tools.

Even the best budgeting apps have the same problem: they're built for categorizing, not deciding. Workbench is focused on how big your budget should be, not how it's sliced up. It forecasts that number forward, so you see what overspending costs you later, and what oversaving costs you now.

Philosophy

Understanding can't be handed to you.

It has to be worked for. Your finances are the same. So I built a tool that rewards doing the work, not skipping it.

One person. Ten years.

Workbench started as a spreadsheet built on a Mint export, because nothing else did what I needed. Ten years and a few hundred iterations later, it's an app — built by one person, in the Midwest. I still use it every month. I'm building the next chapter in public at @GardenStudio__.
